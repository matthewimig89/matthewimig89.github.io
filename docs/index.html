Quantimental Model Philosophy

The philosophy behind this strategy is to use a data-driven approach to understand which fundamental features matter most for predicting stock returns. The model systematically evaluates characteristics across the classic factor dimensions of momentum, quality, and value, without anchoring to any single style.

Because factor performance is not constant over time, the model explicitly accounts for the economic environment. Using macroeconomic data, quarters are segmented into distinct economic regimes (e.g., expansion, slowdown, recession, recovery). Within each regime, the model learns how different features relate to forward returns. This ensures that the strategy adapts to changing conditions rather than relying on static factor weights.

The goal is to combine robust feature engineering with regime-aware modeling to produce a systematic and adaptive stock selection framework grounded in both financial fundamentals and macroeconomic context.

Investment Process

The model is implemented in a systematic, rules-based cycle designed to capture consistent alpha while maintaining diversification and disciplined risk management.

Quarterly Portfolio Construction

Each quarter, the model ranks the universe of stocks using fundamental, momentum, quality, and risk factors. The top 20 stocks are selected, purchased at equal weights (5% each) within that vintage.

One-Year Holding Period

Each quarterly vintage is held for 12 months, ensuring factor exposures have time to play out while reducing short-term trading noise.

Quarterly Rebalancing

At the end of each quarter, the vintage reaching its one-year mark is sold in full. The proceeds are rotated into the newly selected 20-stock portfolio, based on the latest factor rankings.

Ongoing Diversification Across Vintages

At any given time, the portfolio is composed of four vintages, each representing ~25% of total capital. This rolling structure smooths returns, reduces timing risk, and provides continuous exposure to the model's signals.

Multi-Stage Quantitative Stock Selection Model

A multi-stage quantitative stock selection model that adapts to different economic environments. The model identifies four distinct economic regimes using unsupervised clustering and trains separate XGBoost models for each regime to capture regime-specific factor relationships.

Problem Statement

Traditional factor models often fail to account for structural breaks in factor relationships across different economic environments. This model addresses this limitation by explicitly modeling regime changes and adapting factor exposures accordingly.

Business Objective

Generate consistent alpha across market cycles by dynamically adjusting stock selection criteria based on prevailing economic conditions.

Model Components

  • Economic Regime Classifier: K-means clustering on 8 macroeconomic variables
  • Feature Engineering Pipeline: 25+ fundamental and technical indicators with percentile rankings
  • Ensemble Models: Separate XGBoost models for each of 4 economic regimes
  • Validation Framework: Forward-step validation with expanding training windows
  • Performance Attribution: Factor decomposition and regime transition analysis

Data Requirements

Market Data: Daily equity prices & volumes (S&P 500), market cap, sector classifications, corporate actions
Fundamental Data: Quarterly financials, analyst estimates, historical P&L
Macroeconomic Data: GDP growth, unemployment, yield curves, inflation, credit spreads, VIX
